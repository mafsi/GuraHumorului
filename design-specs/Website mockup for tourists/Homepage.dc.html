Southern Bucovina

Where painted monasteries meet ancient forest

A UNESCO landscape of frescoed walls, beech woodland, and centuries of Bucovinian tradition — quiet by nature, and never quite asleep.

Plan your visit Watch the film

Since the 15th century, the monasteries around Gura Humorului have carried their frescoes through winters and centuries alike. Today the town is the quiet gateway to that world — and, most evenings, the market square still smells of woodsmoke and mulled wine.

The "Sistine Chapel of the East" — famed for its cobalt-blue exterior frescoes depicting the Last Judgment. Waymarked routes through old-growth beech forest, with viewpoints over the monastery valleys below. Learn hand-painted egg decoration and wool weaving from artisans keeping the tradition alive.
See the full calendar

Between the frescoes there is a whole valley of festivals, trails, and long suppers — the same restraint, set to a livelier tempo.

Festival

Winter carols in the old market

Each December, colinde singers move door to door in sheepskin and embroidered wool — a tradition older than the town's rail line.

Trail

Sunrise on Obcina Mare

Guided rides and hikes climb through beech and spruce to ridge-top views over every monastery valley at once.

Table

Supper at the old distillery

A restored țuică distillery now serves slow Bucovinian suppers by candlelight, with live violin most Friday evenings.
