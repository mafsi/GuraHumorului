The Living Valley

Bucovina never stops moving — it just never rushes

Festivals, forest trails, and long candlelit suppers, set to the same unhurried tempo as the monasteries themselves.

Festivals & Traditions

The valley keeps its calendar

Bucovina's biggest gatherings follow the church calendar and the harvest, not a tourist season — each one older than the roads that now lead to it.

Winter carol singers in the market square

Winter carols in the old market

Each December, colinde singers move door to door in sheepskin and embroidered wool — a tradition older than the town's rail line.

Painted eggs laid out for Holy Week fair

The painted egg fair

Over Holy Week, artisans from every valley village bring a year of hand-painted eggs to the square — wax, dye, and steady hands.

Wool and harvest fair stalls

Wool & harvest fair

A September gathering of shepherds and weavers, when the summer flocks come down from the ridge pastures for shearing and trade.

Outdoor & Trails

The hills keep their own pace

Waymarked routes through the Obcinele Bucovinei climb gently from monastery to ridge, rewarding an early start with quiet views.

Sunrise ridge view over the monastery valleys

Sunrise on Obcina Mare

Guided rides and hikes climb through beech and spruce to ridge-top views over every monastery valley at once.

Cyclist on a forest road in the Slănic valley

Slănic Valley cycling loop

A half-day loop of quiet forest roads and river crossings, easing back into town in time for supper.

Rafters on the Moldova river in spring

Moldova river run

A gentler stretch of white water each spring, when the snowmelt lifts the river through the pine valley below town.

Food & Craft

Slow tables and working looms

The valley eats and works at the same unhurried pace it prays — long suppers, hand looms, and market days that still set the week.

Candlelit supper inside a restored distillery

Supper at the old distillery

A restored țuică distillery now serves slow Bucovinian suppers by candlelight, with live violin most Friday evenings.

Wool-weaving looms in Ciocănești

The looms of Ciocănești

A village of hand-painted houses and working looms, where weavers still teach the craft on request.

Market stall with cheese and smoked trout

Market-day tasting

Saturday mornings in the old market pair smoked trout from the hills with sheep cheese from the high pastures.
